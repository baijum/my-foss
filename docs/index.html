Malayalam GNU/Linux & SMC

2001–2003

The Malayalam GNU/Linux project aimed to translate X, GNOME, and KDE into Malayalam—the language spoken by 38 million people in Kerala, India. Started as “Malayalam Linux Online” by Baiju M at NIT Calicut, the project later became Swathanthra Malayalam Computing (SMC).

Journey Timeline

2001
Starts “Malayalam Linux Online” community as a student at NIT Calicut. Creates the malayalamlinux Yahoo Group on December 21.
January 2002
Registers the Malayalam GNU/Linux project on SourceForge. Develops GTK+ transliteration input method, Inscript keyboard layouts, and Malayalam Compose files.
October 2002
Submits Malayalam Compose sequences to the GTK+ i18n mailing list. The community adopts the name Swathanthra Malayalam Computing (SMC) and moves to GNU Savannah.
2002–2003
Peak activity: publishes 8 news posts on Savannah covering Malayalam in GNOME 2, GNU Emacs Malayalam support, GPL translation, and free Malayalam fonts.
2004
Begins blogging on LiveJournal and Blogspot. Writes about Python, Zope, and free software advocacy.
2007
Speaks at FOSS.IN 2007 on “Component Software” and “Contributing to Zope.” Contributes a BrowserView patch to GNOME web infrastructure. Active in the BangPypers community.
2007–2012
Authors the ZCA book and the Zope 3 Book. The ZCA book is community-translated into Spanish and French.
2009
Presents “Buildout” at PyCon India 2009. Organizes BangPypers meetups in Bangalore.
2010
Presents “BlueBream” at PyCon India 2010. SMC formalizes as a registered non-profit society.
2011
Delivers a lightning talk at PyCon US 2011 in Atlanta.
2013
Receives the Kenneth Gonsalves Award at PyCon India 2013 for contributions to the Indian Python community.
2016–2017
Conducts Go (Golang) workshops at multiple colleges and venues across India.
