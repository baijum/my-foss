Hi to all,

Last month( June,2003) , Swathantra Software Solutions& Support(
S2S2) had conducted GNU/Linux awareness program at various colleges & schools in Kannur district of Kerala. Talk on " Free Software in Education" and demonstration on GNU/Linux system using Knoppix 3.2 bootable CD was arranged at all places. It was well attended and appreciated by all. The following is the list of
programs and the general impression about it.

1. One day Orientation Programe for school teachers.
Supported & Organized by Kannur M P Shri A P Abdullakutty and the
district administration, the programe was conducted at Chamber
of Commerce Hall at Kannur.
Participants: 115 school teachers from Govt Schools in
Kannur and Wynad participated in the workshop.

2: Seminar on " Free Software,its Philosophy" with GNU/Linux
demo at Mahatma College Iritty, Kannur.
Participants: Faculty & Students of MCA, MSc computer science
3: Seminar and demo on GNU/Linux at " Donbosco College, Angadikadavu,
Participants: Faculty and students
4: Seminar & Demonstration at " Chinmaya Institute of Technology, Kannur
Participants: Faculty and students
5 : Seminar and Demonstration at Chinmaya School, Kannur
Participants, : Teachers, X1 & X11 Students
6: Seminar and Demonstration at Nirmalagiri College, Kuthuparamba
Participants: Faculty, Students

Our feedback about the programe:
---------------------------------
*Programes were successful, in the sense that people at large had
got an idea of what free software is all about and what its impact
will be on society?". Most of the participants knew what "windows" is
but only very few heard about "Linux". After showing them the power of GNU/Linux and how to use it, they were convinced of using the alternate system and its advantage. The "free software philosophy and its "freedom" part is something which the people liked much.

*There are number of teachers who got Intel sponsored master training
certificate. It seems they are trained of using only Microsoft Windows and its products.Though they had initial reservation using GNU/Linux, after seeing the real advantage of free software they
showed interest in trying it out.

*Both students and teachers are overwhelmed by the number of applications and packages available in GNU/Linux.

*Almost all of them, the use of computer is limited to only word processing, spreadsheet, presentation, music and movie playing.

*A common complaint received from many is " It is very slow in my
machine"....

*Students are better in learning and trying out Linux. At Kannur 43 schools had got computers with GNU/Linux installed. Students working on computers first time( no exposure to MS OS) did not find using GNU Linux any difficulty. The common misconception around is that it is not user friendly....The experience shows that, GNU/Linux is more friendly to students than teachers.

*Plus 2 Teachers had different complaint: They would like to try out GNU/Linux in schools. But the school IT course syllabus specify proprietary packages like, " Tally , Visual Basic, MS Office, Oracle, Qbasic etc". Since these are part of syllabus, schools can not shift to GNU/Linux only even if they desire to do so. There are cases where students ask the teachers to get GNU/Linux installed on machines, but school do not want because of syllabus.

*Most schools use proprietary system and packages without
license and they do not actually know such things like license
agreement exist !!!

*** Going to school ( rural and semi-urban area) and telling & showing them the free software education utilities can make a big impact in spreading free software concepts and philosophy.
