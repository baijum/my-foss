"DivX" is arguably the most widely misunderstood term
in computer history. This FAQ tries to dispel some
common myths.

Q: What is DivX?
A: DivX is a codec made by DivXNetworks.

Q: What is a codec?
A: Codec is a piece of software that encodes/decodes
data to/from some format.

Q: Is codec a format?
A: No.

Q: What format/compression does DivX use?
A: DivX is an MPEG-4 (Simple Profile/Advanced Simple
Profile) codec. You can put MPEG-4 video in various
containers, the most common container format is
probably AVI. The official MPEG-4 container format is
MP4.

Q: What is MPEG-4?
A: MPEG-4 is a multimedia standard developed by MPEG.

Q: Is DivX open-source?
A: No, DivX is a commercial closed-source project of
DivXNetworks.

Q: Is DivX the only MPEG-4 codec?
A: No.

Q: Are there open-source MPEG-4 codecs?
A: Yes.

Q: When there are other MPEG-4 codecs, they use the
MPEG-4 video compression, too, like DivX does. So is
it OK to call them "DivX"?
A: No, an MPEG-4 codec is an MPEG-4 codec. They all
have their own names. The name is not (and cannot be)
"DivX", unless it's the one particular codec made by
the DivXNetworks company. You can't call Linux
"Windows", just because it's an operating system.
Different pieces of software made by different people
have different names, even if they implement the same
standard.

Q: Do I need DivX in Linux?
A: No. You can use it with some applications and it's
nice when the option is there. But the option is not
always there. The players and encoders in Linux often
use and support the open-source MPEG-4 codecs. For
example, xine and Avidemux don't support DivX.
However, applications like xine, MPlayer or Avidemux
use built-in libavcodec from FFmpeg for decoding and
encoding. That is, they have built-in FFmpeg MPEG-4,
among other things.

Q: If a player/encoder doesn't support DivX, does it
mean that it can't play/encode MPEG-4 video?
A: No.

Q: I use xine/MPlayer for playback. Do I need to
install anything, any codec, to play MPEG-4 video?
A: No. But if you use the players from your
distribution, some features may be removed due to
legal restrictions. If that's the case, install the
full-featured version from their homepage.
