Do not get the impression that I am trying to justify RedHat  or advocate
its usage.
As for myself, I started with something called SLS (a 3 floppy
distribution), moved to Slackware. Now using
RedHat mostly. Tried Debian, Mandrake and other minor distros like beehive
linux.

Every distro has a place for it. If you know a little bit unix, go for
debian for sure.
If you are trying to switch over from MSWindows to GNU/Linux , RedHat or
Mandrake is fine.

There is another issue worth considering. Both RedHat and Debian follow
the development at a slow pace.
There are centain  features like TruType Font support etc. one like to
have. For that you may need
the latest XFree86.  If you are an expert , you can download and install
it yourself, else you need to look for a distribution
that catches up with the changes faster.
past two days  I have been playing with "Peanut Linux" , only a 250 MB
download  , 10 minutes to install and you have
2.4.19 kernel, XFree86-4.2.99, KDE3 etc. ( a non-free netscape sits there
but RPM for openoffice 1.2 beta is available for peanut)
I could  just install a malayalam TTF font and take a printout with out
any effort.
Honestly I am impressed.

So let us be open to all  of them. A major strength of the Free Software
world is the choice you have.
**Let there be Thousand Distros***
If it sounds like a copy of an old slogan, make it  2000.
