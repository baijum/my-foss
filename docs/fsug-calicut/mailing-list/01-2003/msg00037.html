As a founder member of FSUG, I would like to make the following comments on this
debate.
FSUG has it's own programs and the main one is spreading "Free Software" among
the schools/colleges.
There we are clearly talking about Free Software and not Open Source. "Free
Software" follows the culture
of science, the one of sharing freely.  FSUG has only volunteers and nobody
depends on it for survival.
At the same time I understand the concerns of OSS , where 40 people depends on 
it
and  it's
difficulties with an ill-informed public who think all services related to Free
Software has to be free.

( I myself have come across an official in one of the govt. IT departments who
complain about someone asking for
money to setup a server in Linux. He does not realize how much the organization
saves that way instead of using Window NT
that costs a lot but the dealer may install it for free. )

Most of the FSUG members does not know much about OSS.
We would like to see a listing of the products from OSS along with  some details
on this list rather than the  nature of their  ideological stands.
If it is under GPL, we can put some efforts to take it to people.

regards


ajith
